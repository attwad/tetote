# Care instructions

To ensure safe and long-lasting use of this product, please read the following carefully before use.

## Before First Use

We recommend washing the product with water before its first use.
The product has already undergone a sealing treatment (Mizudome\*1), so it is ready to use immediately. There is no need for traditional "Medome" boiling with rice water.

### Prohibited Items

* Microwave / Dishwasher / Oven: NOT compatible.
* Direct Flame: Prohibited
  Please avoid sudden temperature changes, as this may cause breakage or injury.

### Handling Precautions

* Product Cleaning: Hand wash with a dish detergent and a soft sponge as soon as possible after use .
* No Soaking: Do not soak in water for many hours. This may cause mold, odors, or weaken the structure. Dry thoroughly before storage.
* Bleach: While soaking in bleach may help maintain the original appearance, we do not recommend it due to potential health risks. We recommend you to enjoy the natural patina that develops over time.
* Staining: Due to the nature of ceramics, deep-colored or oily foods may cause staining or odor transfer.
* Damage: This product is delicate. Strong impacts may cause chips or cracks. If any damage occurs, stop use immediately to avoid injury.
* Storage: Keep out of reach of children to prevent breakage from accidental falls.



\*1) **Mizudome** (Water-proofing treatment) is a specialized process used to prevent water absorption and leakage, which can naturally occur due to the porous nature of ceramics. The sealing agent used in this process is safe and complies with strict regulatory standards for food and food additives.

## Characteristics of the Product

Each piece is handcrafted by Japanese artisans, making every item unique.
You can appreciate the following features as the individual character of the product:

* **Individual Differences**: Each piece may have slight variation in shape, size, and weight. These unique characteristics are proof of its “one-of-a-kind” nature of handcrafted artistry.

* **Glaze (Uwagusuri)**: Every piece has a unique application of the glaze, its shading, and natural drip marks. These variations are proof of manual craftsmanship and reflect the beauty of Yohen (natural changes in the kiln). These are stylistic features and do not affect the quality or durability of the product.

* **Kanyu (Crazing)**: Fine, web-like patterns may appear on the surface of the ceramic. This is a characteristic unique to pottery, caused by the difference in shrinkage between the clay and the glaze during the firing process; it is not a crack or a defect in the body itself. As the piece is used over time, you can enjoy a beautiful patina of its color with these lines.

* **Kairagi (Crackled Effect)**: On Shino-glaced ware, a distinct crackled or beaded pattern known as Kairagi may appear. Similar to Kanyu (Crazing), this is a specific characteristic of Shino ware caused by the difference in shrinkage between the clay and the glaze during firing. It is not a structural crack or damage. With long-term use, you can enjoy the evolving beauty of its patina.

* **Pinhole**: Small, needle-like holes may occasionally be visible on the glazed surface. These are natural occurrences caused by the release of air from the clay or glaze during the production process and are a standard characteristic of handcrafted ceramics.

* **Iron Spots (Teppun)**: Small black spots may appear where iron particles inherent in the natural clay or glaze have reacted to the heat. This is a unique texture characteristic of natural materials and artisanal production.
